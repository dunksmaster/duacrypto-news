Most **Albania and Kosovo** readers who ask us “how do I buy Bitcoin?” start on a **centralized exchange**. Bitget is one of the platforms our community uses for first spot purchases — this guide is the **English-localized** companion to our [Albanian Bitget walkthrough](/posts/2026-07-09-bitget-regjistrim-shqiperi-kosove/).

For the full buyer journey, see [How to Buy Bitcoin in Albania](/en/posts/buy-bitcoin-in-albania/).

## Why Bitget (and not random Telegram brokers)

- Regulated-style KYC — expect ID verification.
- Spot BTC pairs with familiar UI for beginners.
- After buying, plan to withdraw to [Tangem](/go/tangem) or another hardware wallet.

**Not financial advice.** Crypto is volatile; only use money you can afford to lose.

## Step 1 — Register

1. Open [Bitget via our referral link](/go/bitget) (supports the project at no extra cost to you).
2. Sign up with email or phone — use one you control long-term.
3. Set a **unique strong password**.
4. Enable **2FA** (Google Authenticator or similar) immediately.

## Step 2 — KYC

Prepare passport or national ID, good lighting for selfie/liveness checks, and patience — verification can take from minutes to a day.

Diaspora readers in **Italy, Germany, or Switzerland** usually verify with documents from their country of residence.

## Step 3 — First deposit and buy

1. Deposit a **small test amount** in EUR/USD (card fees are often higher than bank transfer).
2. Buy **spot BTC** — avoid leverage/futures while learning.
3. Review fees before confirming.

## Step 4 — Withdraw to self-custody

When ready, withdraw a tiny test amount to your hardware wallet address, confirm on-chain, then move the rest. Read our [Tangem guide for Albanian readers](/en/posts/tangem-hardware-wallet-albania/) (EN) or the [Albanian Tangem post](/posts/2026-07-09-portofol-harduerik-tangem-shqiptaret/).
